Mobile App

Sproot AR Wayfinding

A modern wayfinding solution for grocery stores, focusing on seamless shopping experience and customer engagement.

Timeline

16 Weeks

Role

UX/UI Designer

Tools

Figma Adobe Illustrator VS Code

Challenge

In-person grocery shopping has not kept up with our fast-paced lives, rising prices, and increasing dietary restrictions. Shoppers need a better way to find their everyday essentials without wandering aisles or reading endless labels.

  • Unintuitive store navigation
  • Lack of personalized recommendations
  • Information overload when comparing deals

Solution

A intuitive mobile app with Augmented Reality wayfinding, personalized recommendations, and seamless shopping experience.

  • Easy navigation to items on the users shopping list using AR way finding
  • Personalized recommendations based on shopping history and preferences
  • Seamless integration with store loyalty programs, deals, and rewards

Process & Approach

Research

To understand the problem, my field study took place at a local grocery store. Research methods included:

  • A competitive analysis of the grocery store app and its competitors
  • User interviews with grocery shoppers

Competitive Analysis

Google Lens

Technology: Google Lens is a visual search tool that uses augmented reality (AR) and computer vision to recognize and provide information about objects in the real world when viewed through a smartphone camera.

Application: Similar to Sproot, Google Lens allows users to scan and identify items, landmarks, or objects. It can provide information, translate text, and perform various other tasks based on the visual input. It empowers users with knowledge and context in real-time.

Hardware: Android and some iOS smartphones with a camera

Software: Integrated into the Google app on Android and available as a standalone app on iOS.

Platform: iOS and Android

IKEA Place

Technology: IKEA Place is an AR app that allows users to visualize how IKEA furniture would look and fit in their own space by superimposing 3D models of furniture into their surroundings through the smartphone camera.

Application: While different in focus, IKEA Place shares a common use of AR in the context of retail. It helps customers make informed purchasing decisions by allowing them to see how a product fits into their home environment, similar to how Sproot uses AR to inform and inspire in the grocery shopping context.

Hardware: Android and iOS smartphones with a camera

Software: ARKit (for iOS), ARCore (for Android)

Platform: iOS and Android
