My Process

Great design comes from a thoughtful, flexible approach. My process centres on understanding the people I'm designing for, always with the users needs in mind.

Step 1

Discovery & Research

My design journey kicks off with deep-dive research, gathering insights about users, the business, and the market. Connecting every insight to uncover those important core user needs. Combining all of this creates the essential blueprint for impactful design.

Step 2

Ideation & Wireframing

With the research insights in hand, I start creating the experience, by developing the information architecture and sketch out low-fidelity wireframes. This step is crucial for clearly defining how users will use the product and their core journey. It ensures everything is intuitive and usable before any visual elements are introduced.

Step 3

Visual Design

Once the structural foundation is set, I start working on the visual aspect. Turning wireframes into high-fidelity designs, to build that cohesive visual language that communicates the brands identity. This means creating something that looks great and aligns with the brand, but also enhances usability and an emotional connection with users.

Step 4

Prototyping & Testing

After Visual Design, I develop interactive prototypes to simulate the user experience. Putting these to the test through usability testing, gathering direct feedback. This crucial step validates design decisions and brings to light key opportunities for improvements, making sure the product meets core user needs.

Step 5

Implementation & Iteration

My work doesn't stop at the hand-off. Even after launch, I continue to gather feedback and data, using these insights to make thoughtful improvements and iterate on the experience.
